Discover San Lorenzo

The city of San Lorenzo is the 4th most populated city in Paraguay. It has a population of around 260,000 people, but because of its intense commercial activity, nearly a million people enter and leave it during the day.

Catedral de San Lorenzo

Built at the beginning of the XX Century, the Cathedral is one of the must-visit places in the city. Its neo-gothic style (unique in the country), its clock (first building-sized clock in Paraguay), and its beautiful ceiling makes of it a wonderful historic and artistic site.

Mercado

The San Lorenzo Street Market is one of the largest and most varied market in the country. Suppliers from all places in Paraguay gather in this market to offer fruits, vegetables, dairy products, honey, street food, herbs, spices, clothing, and a thousand other products and services.

Ciclovia

With native plants and trees, the Ciclovia is a 100-acre park where nature and city blend. Whether you would like to walk, run, or ride a bike in a place with clean air and abundant vegetation, this is a perfect spot. It also has a pond with ducks and geese, where you can sit to enjoy the place.

Universidad Nacional

The largest and most important university campus in the country is located in San Lorenzo, right next to the Ciclovia. With hundreds of buildings and labs, this is a place to visit to learn about the education system of the country, and also to discover the routine and lifestye of young adults.

Cuartel de la Victoria

The Chaco War was a conflict between Paraguay and Bolivia that took place during the 1930's, and this place is a memorial in remembrance of all those men and women that gave their lives in this war. It has many statues, plaques, and guides that give details about the war.
